ASME SDC Project

Background

The ASME-SDC (Student Design Competition) is a project hosted by ASME (American Society of Mechanical Engineers.) The competition was created to be held at the ASME E-Fest, which was an engineering festival which promised to let me "party like an engineer", with food, a concert and lots of interesting lightning talks from industry professionals. The robot was no party to design, however. The competition was a robot pentathalon, which meant that the robot had to complete 5 different tasks. The tasks were the hit, the throw, the sprint, the climb, and the lift. While we were there, we had a lot of fun, placed better than we expected, and even learned a thing or two along the way.

The Design Process

The first step of this project was some intense brainstorming when the rules first came out. This took place over the course of months, and honestly was cause for a lot of problems with the robot down the road. When desigining the robot, we should have worked together in our large group to design each portion together. Instead, we broke into teams of 4 and each pushed to design a subsystem for the robot. In theory, this allowed us to complete the robot much faster. In reality, it discouraged communication between groups, which led to oversights while designing, and led to an inferior robot.

Solidworks

Being a freshman coming from a prepratory high school, I had had 0 previous CAD experience. Also, considering the project started early into the fall semester, I didn't even have the chance to take the introductory CAD CAM class offered at my school. Because of this, I was forced to learn Solidworks by trial an error, asking upperclassman questions and fiddling around with it every chance I got. through a few iterations, I designed a scissor lift to complete lift portion of the competition

Manufacturing

Manufacturing the SDC robot was my first experience working with aluminum. Today, working with aluminum isn't too worrisome, because I've realized it cuts incredibly easily. This project definitely taught me about the very basics of manufacturing, something I continue to learn about on both the ROV project and my Manufacturing 1000 class.
